BYTES

Our Mission

Collaborating to solve complex social problems with technology

Bytes creates a space for developers, designers, data geeks, leaders, and idea-makers together to collaboratively ideate, create and invent new solutions using community expertise, publicly- released data, code and technology to improve our communities. Anyone can participate; you don’t have to be an expert in technology, you just have to care about your neighborhood and community.

Events will range from idea dinners, to hackathons, to meetups, to “pitches” with government entities and technologists, all the while building a community that cares deeply about the city and serves as an intellectual powerhouse for those building it. Together, we look at the role technology can play in catalyzing change in sectors ranging from financial services to veterans benefits. We’ll focus on local problems that millions of people in America face, partnering with New York non-profits and community service agencies as a testing ground for potential solutions.

First Event: Making Healthcare More Accessible

This January, Bytes will partner with Robin Hood Foundation to host an idea dinner that looks at how we can use large data sets provided by auntbertha.com to make quality, affordable healthcare more accessible.

The evening will start off with context, followed by a discussion lead by experts who work to make healthcare accessible for their clients. We will then brainstorm over dinner to discuss possible technical solutions.
